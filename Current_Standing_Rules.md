# Current Standing Rules

Last updated: 25 June 2026.

## Aim

King's Crossing is a turn-based board game for two sides.

Player 1 controls the White Pieces and tries to guide the king upward through
the crossing. Player 2 controls the Black Pieces and tries to block, trap, or
seal the king before it escapes.

## Main Crossing

- Black moves first.
- Black places one new piece on the top row.
- Black pieces enter in a repeating cycle: pawn, knight, bishop.
- White then moves the king to a legal dot.
- A forward king move advances the pawn wall from below.
- If Royal Jump moves the king upward by two rows, the pawn wall advances by
  two rows as well.
- Sideways king moves do not move the pawn wall up by two rows.
- Black wins if the top row becomes sealed.
- Black wins if the king is trapped, caught by the pawn wall, or moves into a
  dangerous square.

## Black Pieces

- Pawns are blocking pressure from the top row.
- Knights control L-shaped squares.
- Bishops control diagonal lines.
- Black pieces can defend each other.
- The king can capture an undefended Black piece, but not a defended piece.

## White Pieces

- The king normally moves to the nearby legal dots shown on the board.
- Eagle Vision reveals danger squares.
- Royal Jump gives the king a wider one-move leap when charged.
- White is the harder side because it must keep finding a safe path while the
  board pressure grows.

## Final Duel

- After 12 upward turns, the royal guard appears and the Grand Regent Queen
  enters the board.
- White wins by touching the row directly beneath the royal guard.
- That escape row wins immediately, even if the queen controls the square.
- Black controls the queen and may move her along queen lines.
- Queen's Wrath lets Black add a rook to tighten the net around the king.
- The rook must be placed on a legal empty square and must not instantly check
  the king.

## Single Player

- The game can be played as two-player or single-player.
- In single-player mode, the human chooses White or Black.
- The AI controls the opposite side and chooses legal moves from the game
  module.
